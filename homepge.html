Live West-Bengal through its festivals

Saraswati Puja

This festival happens in the period between January and February. Bengalis celebrate this day as the homecoming of the Goddess Saraswati. This festival is celebrated on the 5thday of Magha month. It is also called as Vasant Panchami in Northern areas of the country. It is considered to be a highly fortunate occasion for their children to begin their academic learning.

Durga Puja

This festival happens in the period between September and October. The massive celebration that happens during the festival is famous throughout the world. Goddess Durga defeated the demon “Mahishasura” after a fierce battle that lasted for ten days. The last day or tenth day is called as Vijay Dashmi.

Dol Purnima

This is the Bengali version of the Holi festival. Dol Purnima is celebrated by men and women in a very dignified manner. It is done by placing the statues of Krishna and Radha on an appealingly adorned palanquin that is taken around the major streets of the village or the city.

Id-Ul-Fitr

Eid is another very significant festival in West Bengal. It is celebrated mostly by the Muslims in the state. This festival memorializes the commencement of a joyful-communion post a month of fasting and prayer. Both non-Muslims and Muslims gather in large numbers in the Kolkata Maidan to witness and enjoy this festival.

Kali Puja

Kali Puja festival is celebrated in the honor of Goddess Kali. It is recognized by the chanting of mantras in the nighttime that goes till dawn. The Goddess is ornated with beautiful garlands made of hibiscuses. All the sacred chanting of mantras is carried out in the presence of the holy fire.

Dive in to the divine world

Kalighat

A popular religious attraction of Kolkata, Kalighat Kali Temple is considered as one of the 52 Shakti Peethas of India. Kalighat is said to be the site where the toes of the right foot of Sati fell.

Dakshineshwar

Dakshineshwar Kali Temple is located on the Eastern banks of Hoogly river near Kolkata. The temple has a large complex containing 12 Shiva Temples, Radha-Krishna temple, Rani Rashmoni Temple and a river ghat. The temple is built in Nava Ratna style of Bengal architecture which has 9 towers and 3 storeys.

Tarapit

Tārāpīṭh is a Hindu temple in Rampurhat subdivision of Birbhum district of the Indian state of West Bengal, known for its Tantric temple and its adjoining cremation (Maha Shashan) grounds where sādhanā (tantric rituals) are performed. Tarapith the chief temples of Shaktism derives its name from its association as the most important centre of Tara worship

Belur Mutt

Belur Mutt or Belur Math is the main centre of Ramkrishna Mission. It was set by Swami Vivekananda which played a major role in Ramakrishna Movement in India. The temple architecture is special as it signifies the unity and diversity of all religions. Today, it is one of the major tourist places that allure visitors in West Bengal.

Iskon Temple

Mayapur is believed as the birth place of Chaitanya Mahaprabhu It is the headquarters of ISKCON Temples in India. Srila Prabhupada's (founder of ISKCON) memorial is the main attraction in Mayapur. This samadhi mandir gets thousands of visitors year round

Chilling in Bengal!

New Market

This is one of the oldest markets in Kolkata which is famous for its variety of shopping items. With over 2,000 stalls available for shopping, this place is one of the best when it comes to finding some eye-catchy and innovative stuff. You can find wide varieties of sarees and other clothing materials. It is also the place where in the evening most of the local sellers display their stalls for local jewellery and different kinds of bags. As this place is a treat to the bargainers, you must expect a large crowd, especially in the evening.

  • Location: Lindsay Street
  • Things to buy: Clothes, bags, household items
  • Timings: Monday to Friday 10 am to 8 pm

Bara Bazaar

Bara Bazaar in Kolkata can be very relatable to the busy markets of Delhi like Chandni Chowk. This market is divided into several regions as it offers a vast variety of everything that you would need. Right from spices, electronic items, cosmetics, home essentials, toys to artificial jewellery and textiles, this place can offer you almost everything. If you are thinking that such variety would cost you lots then to a surprise, this market is extremely cheap and affordable.

  • Location: Near to Mahatma Gandhi Road Metro Station
  • Things to buy: All the daily essentials
  • Timings: 10:30 am to 7:30 pm, Sunday: closed

Howrah Phool Bazaar

Flowers can add charm to any decorations, isn’t it? If you too are in a need to buy flowers for any of your occasions and are looking for the best place where you can get a huge variety of flowers then this flower market of Kolkata is your destination. No matter what your need is, you will surely find that easily in this market. This whole seller marketplace for flowers has a large variety of flowers and offers that at an affordable price.

  • Location: Eastern side of Howrah Bridge
  • Things to buy: Fresh flowers
  • Timings: 4 am to 10 pm

Lava

A journey of about 100 km from Siliguri via Kalimpong or 115 km via Gorubathan will take you to Lava, one of the most picturesque tourist destinations in the Darjeeling Himalayas. Lava is a serene misty hamlet in the Kalimpong sub-division, Lava has been a more recent addition in the tourism map of North Bengal and is getting immense popularity among the travelers looking for a tourist destination off the beaten track. Situated at an altitude of 2100 m, Lava presents a landscape with ultimate splendor of nature that can never be captured by word. The conifer forests under the warm glow of sun with colorful birds flying and singing everywhere, the whole surrounding will enthrall you to a new dimension.

Knowledge hub

Victorial Memorial

The Victoria Memorial is a large marble building in Kolkata, West Bengal, India, which was built between 1906 and 1921. It is dedicated to the memory of Queen Victoria, then Empress of India, and is now a museum under the auspices of the Ministry of Culture. The memorial lies on the Maidan (grounds) by the bank of the Hooghly River

Science City

The Science City of Kolkata is the largest science centre in the Indian subcontinent. It is Managed by National Council of Science Museums (NCSM), Ministry of Culture, Government of India. It comprises Caterpillar Ride, Gravity Coaster, Musical Fountain, Road Train, Cable Cars, Monorail Cycle, butterfly nursery and several exhibits on physical and life sciences and a maze set up in a lush green ambience.

William Fort

Fort William, citadel of Calcutta, named for King William III of England. Between 1696 and 1702 a fort was built in Calcutta, with the nawab (ruler) of Bengal’s permission. The Fort is built of brick and mortar in the shape of an irregular octagon with an area of 5 km2 (1.9 sq mi). Five of its sides face landward, and three towards the Hooghly River. The design is that of a star fort, suited to defence against cannon firing solid shot, and dates from before the advent of explosive shells.

Shantinikethan

Shantiniketan is a neighbourhood of Bolpur city in Bolpur subdivision of Birbhum district in West Bengal, India, approximately 152 km north of Kolkata . It was established by Maharshi Devendranath Tagore, and later expanded by his son Rabindranath Tagore whose vision became what is now a university town with the creation of Visva-Bharati. Santiniketan is known world over for its contribution to education, arts, and literature.
